Tim Gauthier—Guitar

Tim started playing guitar as a teenager in Lincoln Nebraska, and it has been his passion ever since.  His musical taste is eclectic, and he draws from a diverse palette of musical experience.   Bands he has played with include The Devil's Thumb Bluegrass Band, Touch Monkeys, Close to Home, Hugh Regan & Marc Sabatella, T-Band, Cowtown Boogie, and The Mongomery Jazz Group.  Tim was the 2001 guitar champion at the Rockygrass Bluegrass Festival and has competed in the National Flat-Picking Championships in Winfield, Kansas several times.  While completing his degree in music education at Colorado State University, he enjoyed playing in different jazz ensembles. For over twenty years, he has played as a member or side man in a variety of groups and especially likes the fresh sound of the jazz trio.   Whether he is practicing, playing with friends in his living room, or performing for an audience, he loves the  satisfaction that comes from getting it “just right.”  Playing with Colorado front range musicians has helped him better understand this great American art form, given him a chance to challenge his level of musicianship, and provided him an opportunity to meet a lot of really fun people.

John Johnson—Bass

Bassist John Johnson was born and raised in Westport Connecticut where he started his  musical career as bassist for blues bands  working the New England circuit. Since moving to Colorado in the 1980’s, his extensive performing, recording and touring work have afforded him the rare opportunity to become an accomplished musician. Years of playing nightly before a wide range of audiences and clubs have served to hone John’s abilities to communicate and express himself musically. John has worked with many great players:  John Sansone, Hugh Ragin, Marc Sabatella, Greg Carol, Max Wagner and Blues legend Homesick James Williamson to name a few. John's bass style can be described as  supportive, sensitive and soulful with a unique sound all his own.

Chuck Landgraf—drums

Chuck first got hooked on jazz while listening to his dad's big band record collection. He went to Wichita State University, where he played in a couple of jazz bands and graduated with a B.A. in marketing. Since 1987, he has lived in Colorado.  Chuck has played with a number of Colorado jazz musicians, including Hugh Ragin, Marc Sabatella, Mark Sloniker, and Walt  Jenkins.  He often plays with Mark Sloniker and teaches percussion through the Faith Academy of Fine Arts in Fort Collins and plays classical  percussion/music in the Faith Evangelical Free Church orchestra. He plays jazz for the love of it, and he is very, very good. "Chuck's playing kicks butt" says one fellow musician. Who could ask for a better tribute?
